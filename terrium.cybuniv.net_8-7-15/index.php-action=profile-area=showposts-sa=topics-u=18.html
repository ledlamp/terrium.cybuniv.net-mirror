Updates/Announcements / An updated To-Do list, and general information about development
« on: August 10, 2013, 05:16:39 AM »
Hello!


I am aware that no-one has had any updates from the development team since Eric and I stopped updating the forums on Mars. Well... here's some information for you.


Firstly, the reason we've had very slow progress: vacations. Eric's been away for a few weeks, and has hardly been around over the past month. GTR vanished part-way through making the new tank, in order to "set up for college" if I remember correctly - which is a fair excuse. So that's left me alone to work on the project, and I'll be honest, I've taken a few breaks of one or two days every now and again.


The current status: Well... basic networking works. Positions sync across all computers in a server, and lasers fire when (and sometimes where) they're supposed to. The physics of the buggy has been revamped a few times, to make it more realistic and to improve high-speed handling.


And the future: Rather than type out a lengthy statement, I'm just going to paste our current ToDo list. It's a very short-term thing. I personally hope to have all the things mentioned on it checked off by the end of the month.



Networking:
1. Reported not working. Unlikely to be the case but I want to check over it, just in case.
2. Lasers - collision and sync is not working
3. Glitchy collision physics? Needs looking into, again.


3D Modeling:
1. Buggy needs a floor √ (Done)
2. Tank needs finishing
3. Jet needs starting


Graphics:
1. Underwater effects needed


Music:
1. Finish Menu Piece
2. Start Theme Piece




Finally, I'm quitting development for a few months, starting from September, for exams. I'll be back after the exam period, which means I'll be working again in December, hopefully.


In the meantime, we continue to update the Alpha build (available on the cybuniv site, just follow the signs).

Development / Mars 2.5 Source
« on: June 07, 2013, 01:24:52 AM »
Firstly, I'd like to thank Eric for making it possible for me to post again

The source code is now here!

A few things before you attempt to run it - firstly, the multitudes of errors are all ok. Unity tells you that you can't call RandomRangeInt outside the main thread; well, you can. The other error - concerning colours - is stupid too, it finds the material colour needed then reports that it doesn't exist.

One last thing: if you choose to test-run this in the editor (or in a build) DO NOT use the "Stop Hosting" button to leave a world, always use the editor's play button (or command/control-q in an application). If you press the button, the game/editor will freeze. This is because it tries to disconnect from a master server it never connected to.

So, here's the product:

https://www.dropbox.com/s/iqpo89jpxes57s7/Mars%20Explorer%202.5%20Source.zip

Enjoy, it should run happily in Unity 4. Feel free to modify to your heart's content, and don't forget that the worlds are now cached when you load them.

Credit goes to:
- Eric, for being a fantastic project leader, and for working on the code
- _ACE_ (me!), for fixing and modifying whirld (and otherwise just coding)
- Abister, for testing the project on his Windows machine just before the release… the cache wouldn't work on Windows but for his help.
- The members of the Mars Skype group, for their support and feedback
- The Mars community, for putting up with us

If you have any questions or concerns about the code, please send me a PM, I'll be glad to help wherever I can.
