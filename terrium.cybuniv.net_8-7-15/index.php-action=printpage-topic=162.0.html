Two days ago (July 2nd, 2015) we went to the Habitat Restore (we also went yesterday but didn't really get anything). There were a lot of good stuff in and around the bin of free stuff. There were three big weatherproof utility boxes (brand new) and I got all of those, and there was also an outdoor table with a glass cover which we also got. I found an IKEA 11w CFL down at the bottom.


They recently reorganized their stuff and now it is terrible. Before there was a bin full of CFLs, a bin full of halogen bulbs, a bin full of giant metal halide bulbs and other HID bulbs, and lots of other good stuff. Now they don't have any of that stuff, and I could barely find any halogen bulbs anywhere! All they have now is a shelf with lots of crap bulbs but not much else. There were no low-voltage halogen bulbs anywhere which was what I was looking for. There were a bunch of high-voltage halogen bulbs though.


Anyway, right when we were about to leave, I looked through the bin of free stuff to find anything new, and right next to the bin the clerk was sorting through a paper bag of light bulbs. I immediately spotted a yellow bug light CFL  ;D  and other cool CFLS. Then she walked into the office/counter area (whatever it's called) for a moment, and then walked back and put some/most/all of the bulbs into the bin of free stuff!! YAY!! There was a 6-pack of 100 wat incandescent bulbs but I didn't take that. I took all the CFLs.


So these are the CFLs I got. These are the info directly copied from the text on the lamp.

Yeah. See, CFLs are dirt cheap now and are often given away for free because many people are switching to LEDs making CFLs "old technology" and people are getting rid of them. And also because there have been tons of CFLs produced, but if LED lamps weren't popular or didn't even exist, CFLs would be worth more because they are the latest technology.

But CFLs aren't really bad. LEDs aren't much more efficient than CFLs, so CFLs are a really cheap and easy way to get a lot of light! Only problem with them is that they contain mercury which is why many people don't want them, but that is ok as long as you be careful.
