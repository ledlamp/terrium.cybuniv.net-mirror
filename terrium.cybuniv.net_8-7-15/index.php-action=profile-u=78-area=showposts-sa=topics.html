Creations / Chase game
« on: July 05, 2015, 11:11:19 PM »
Me and Fios have invented a fun game to play in Terrium.
It's called Chase and it is self-explanatory.

Here is how to play:


This is a two-player game. I don't know if it would work with 3 players.


First start a game in Ace's Canyon. Ace's Canyon is the best world for playing chase, but if you want to experiment you can try other worlds.


Now, the goal of the game is very simple. All you do is chase the quarry and try to get quarry. Then when you are quarry, the opponent will chase you.


When you are quarry, run away from the opponent to try to prevent them from catching you.
When the opponent catches you and becomes quarry, the roles reverse, and you chase the opponent while they try to avoid you.


You can fly, but try not to fly too much. Just sometimes fly to dart or keep on the ground or to bridge gaps. This is supposed to be chasing, not dogfighting. But you could occasionally start a short dogfight, then get back to chasing.


You are allowed to shoot as much as you want.


And that is pretty much all. If you want there to be an ending, you can set a max score, so the first person to reach that score will win. Or, you could use timers, and measure the amount of time each person has quarry, and the first person to reach the max wins.




This game works very well in Terrium because of Terrium's wonky physics. It's challenging to keep your buggy going straight because the buggy tends to spin out of control (like if you were driving backwards). You can take advantage of this by darting the other direction as soon as you spin 180˚ so you zoom past your opponent in the opposite direction so they have to turn around.
Also, the buggies take a long time to accelerate, so when you spin out of control, it takes a while to get back on track. And once you're at full speed, it's fun, and challenging because you have to try and keep your speed without loosing it.




We think this should be one of the game modes to choose, so there could be special features in the game just for chasing.


Here is a video of me playing chase with firespeed:
http://youtu.be/w2L_JhR8bLs
It didn't come out very well because after I started recording, Terrium was a bit more laggy for me, and also firespeed started glitching making it hard to shoot him. But it still shows you how it works.

Development / Multiplayer is messed up (workaround)
« on: July 05, 2015, 05:22:15 PM »
It is very hard to join someone's game and have the terrain work. Usually what happens is the first time it gets stuck at loading asset dependencies or something like that, then afterwards it would get stuck at initilizing game or something like that. To fix this you host a game, exit, and join again, and usually you can get in, but then the terrain doesn't work.


I found a way to get in someone's game AND have the terrain work, the first time! Here is how to do it:
  • Restart Terrium
  • Host a game
  • Exit your hosted game (you'd probably get stuck there staring at the sky anyway)
  • Join the game you want
You should be able to get in the game and the terrain should work.


Edit: I have found that this work-around doesn't always work. To make it work you should host the same world as the world of the server that you want to join. Sometimes you may host, exit, and then join the game, but it still doesn't work, but then host a game again with the same world and you will actually end up in that game, and hopefully without missing terrain.

Here are all the lamps in a box/tray:



Here is the Ikea lamp. This was the first one I got. Interestingly I found another lamp just like this for sale on the shelf of light bulbs. Why did they give this one away for free but sell the other one? They're crazy.



Here is the yellow Commercial Electric anti-bug light. The tube inside wiggles around a bit when I shake it but that is no big deal. It appears to have been used a lot. This is an old one.



Here is the Philips Marathon lamp. Philips Marathon Universal bulbs like this are old and rather rare. They are very good quality and are notorious for long life.



Here is the GE 13w CFL. The top plastic part is blue which made me think it was a daylight CFL (high color temperature, cooler white) but it actually is not. It's probably a GE Reveal CFL so they make the top blue because the original GE Reveal incandescent bulbs were blue like that when off. So if this is a Reveal CFL then it probably has a higher color temperature than 2700K—probably 3000K.



Here are the Satco CFLs. I have two of these. They are GU24 bulbs but I don't have any GU24 sockets to use them yet. One of them does not have a box and one does. The one that does not have a box has had quite some use because the plastic is a bit yellowed. The other one appears to not have been used at all, or at least used very little. It is clean and has no yellowing or anything.



Here is the Greenlite CFL. Now here's something really interesting about this. On it there is a PG&E sticker:

Notice the date. This is from 2007. How interesting. So I guess this was a demo CFL to get people to use CFLs back in 2007?
Interesting thing about this bulb is that, for 13w, it outputs 900 lumens, and the tube is longer (making the spiral taller), and the bulb is a bit heavier than usual.



Now, here is the interesting bulb. This is the reason why I posted a post about light bulbs here on Terrium forums.
Well, this is a Cyber Lamp! Yep! Get it? It is related to me and Eric, because I am Lamp and Eric is TheCyberMan. It also has a relation to CarbonTech, because the company who made this is called Cyber Tech. Hehe! How cool is that!
Here is their website: http://www.cybertechlighting.com/
Another cool thing about this CFL is that it is 4100K (called Daylight or Cool White). Daylight CFLs are kinda rare. The most popular is soft white/warm white (2700K) which is what you see everywhere. It is a yellowish white, and daylight is a bluish white.



And we also got one incandescent bulb. This is a 60w frosted candelabra bulb.
